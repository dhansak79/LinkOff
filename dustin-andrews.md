
## Words and symbols
# dead giveaways for AI writing
- Never use emdash (—)
- Never use ore than 3 emoji types. ✅, and ❌ are acceptable sparingly. 

# Overused emphasis words. Usually removable without losing meaning. Includes but not limited to:
- simply, basically, clearly, obviously, certainly, indeed, actually, uncomfortable, truth


### Voice Killers (Never Use)

**Corporate poison**: optimization, approach, framework, technique, methodology, systematic, scaling, leverage, holistic

**Consultant speak**: "The [Noun]" framing, "Here's what [group] doesn't tell you", "The bottom line", "The brutal truth", "Everyone says/told me".

**Inspirational garbage**: "This isn't X, this is Y", "That's not failure, that's learning", "Everything you need to know"

**Punctuation fraud**: Colons for drama, double asterisks, slide-deck bullets, EM-dashes

**AI buzzword bingo**: agentic, breakthrough, game-changing

### Filler hooks that signal obvious reveal
These precede information that stands on its own. The content is useful — don't dress it up with faux-revelation language.
- "The fix:" / "The solution:"
- "Here's how:"
- "The secret:"
- "What you need to do:"
- "The key insight:"
- "Why this works:"
- "The bottom line:"
- Dash where a period works grammatically

## Phrases - Includes but not limited to
### Filler that signals an obvious statement
Any phrase that precedes an obvious statement as if it were profound. These are signs the writer is about to state something obvious in a way that sounds important.
- At the end of the day
- The brutal/honest truth
- But here is the thing / Here's the thing
- Bottom line
- Long story short
- That said / That being said
- What it comes down to
- When all is said and done
- Make no mistake
- "And here is the one nobody talks about enough" (awkward phrasing)
- you cannot unsee it
- here is the thing

### Bait hooks and false revelations
Headlines that rely on exaggeration or fake controversy to get clicks.
- Any hook like "(And Why Most of You Are Lying About Where You Are)"
- "What You Should Actually Worry About"
- "Let's be real"
- "The Uncomfortable Truth"
